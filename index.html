01. Reading Passage

Cyberbullying is the use of digital communication to ignore, harass, or threaten others. It often happens through social media, chat rooms, or private messages. Unlike physical bullying, it can follow a victim home, making them feel unsafe even in their own bedroom.

The most important rule is to never keep it a secret. Finding proof of the bullying, such as screenshots, is vital for the police or school authorities to take action. You must remember that it is never your fault.

The Digital Ripple Effect

The emotional impact of these digital attacks can create a "ripple effect," damaging a student's confidence and mental health. However, digital responsibility starts with the bystander. If you see someone being targeted, reporting the behavior is a sign of strength, not weakness.

Every message sent leaves a permanent footprint. By choosing to act responsibly, we protect the digital environment for everyone. Remember: A single report can stop the cycle of harassment before it escalates.
